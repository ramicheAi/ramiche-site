## Consensus Recommendations (3+ agents agreed)

1. **Tiered certification is essential** — All 5 agents proposed multi-level certification (Bronze/Silver/Gold or Tier 1/2/3). Consensus: graduated trust levels with escalating requirements.

2. **Annual recertification required** — All 5 specified ongoing verification, not one-time certification.

3. **Insurance is non-negotiable** — KIYOSAKI, SELAH, and THEMIS all require E&O / malpractice insurance ($1M–$5M per agent).

4. **Platform fee should be 15-30%** — MERCURY: 15%, KIYOSAKI: 20%, SELAH: 30%. Needs standardization.

5. **Human oversight for high-stakes verticals** — SELAH and THEMIS both require human professional supervision at higher tiers. KIYOSAKI requires fiduciary compliance.

6. **Start with least-regulated verticals** — Sales (MERCURY) and security (WIDOW) have the fewest regulatory hurdles. Legal and psychology have the most.

## Top 5 Strategic Takeaways

1. **Lead with sales, not legal.** MERCURY's vertical has zero regulatory barriers and proves the marketplace model. Legal is the flagship but shouldn't be the first to market.

2. **Platform fee: standardize at 20%.** Split the difference between MERCURY (15%) and SELAH (30%). KIYOSAKI's 20% with the 5% escrow/insurance fund is the best model.

3. **The Atlanta meeting is about clearing the UPL gate.** Everything else is secondary. If the lawyer confirms safe harbor categories exist and a "legal technology platform" positioning works, the legal vertical is a go.

4. **Insurance before launch, not after.** Every responding agent flagged E&O/malpractice insurance as critical. Budget $50-100K for platform-level coverage before accepting any clients.

5. **Delaware C-Corp from day one.** KIYOSAKI's recommendation is sound — investor-friendly, clean equity, 21% flat rate, Florida ops HQ for no state income tax. Don't waste time on LLC conversion later.
