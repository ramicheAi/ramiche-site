## 1. Vision

A nature-rooted, tech-forward restaurant and social lounge that fuses Caribbean, Asian, and North African cuisine with a cannabis consumption experience — set inside a living greenhouse. TREéS isn't a restaurant with plants. It's a botanical world with food, smoke, music, and art woven through it.

**One-liner:** Farm-to-table fusion cuisine inside a living greenhouse with a licensed cannabis lounge, live music, and raw juicery — nature meets future, Wynwood, Miami.

---

## 2. Design Philosophy

### Aesthetic DNA
- **70s/80s bungalow loft** meets **biophilic futurism**
- Natural materials (reclaimed wood, stone, rattan, terracotta) fused with smart glass, LED grow lighting, and living walls
- Christmas-style string lights overhead creating constellation patterns across the greenhouse ceiling
- Floor-to-ceiling windows — maximum natural sunlight during the day
- Bonsai trees as table centerpieces and spatial dividers
- Moroccan-influenced lounge seating in the smoke room — low cushions, ornate textiles, brass tea service
- Murals by rotating local Wynwood artists across all interior walls

### Design Tension
The concept lives in the space between **organic warmth** and **technological precision**. Every surface should feel alive (moss walls, hanging plants, water features) while the infrastructure is invisible and cutting-edge (automated climate control, app-integrated ordering, dynamic lighting that shifts with time of day).

## 3. Patron Journey & Open Floor Plan

### Design Principle
**One continuous space, no walls between zones.** Patrons move through TREéS like walking through a botanical garden — each area flows into the next. Transitions are defined by changes in floor material, ceiling height, lighting, and plant density — never by walls. Every angle is considered. Every sightline reveals something worth looking at.

### The Journey (front to back)

**ARRIVAL — The Canopy Entrance**
- Street-facing glass facade, 16ft ceilings, massive pivot doors (wood + glass)
- Living wall on the right — floor-to-ceiling vertical garden, first thing you see
- Host station built from a reclaimed tree trunk — natural, sculptural, functional
- The entire interior is visible from the entrance. You see the greenhouse, the art, the kitchen, the stage. You know immediately: this place is different.

**ZONE 1 — The Cafe & Juicery (Street-Accessible)**
- Left side of entrance, its own flow — doesn't require entering main dining
- Espresso bar, pour-over, cold brew, matcha, raw pressed juices, smoothie bowls, wellness shots
- Light menu: sandwiches, grain bowls, pastries
- Morning-to-afternoon (7AM-4PM), transitions to cocktail bar at night
- Grab-and-go window for Wynwood foot traffic
- Materials: polished concrete floor, light wood counter, hanging terracotta planters
- This zone catches the walk-in crowd and funds morning operations

**ZONE 2 — The Gallery & Waiting Lounge**
- Between entrance and main dining — where you wait, where you linger, where you buy
- **THE FOCAL ART WALL** — 30ft wide, 14ft tall, gallery-grade lighting. Rotating installations by Wynwood artists. Purchasable. QR codes link to artist profiles + pricing.
- Opposite wall: curated smaller pieces, sculptures on floating shelves, limited prints
- Low seating — rattan lounge chairs, stone side tables, bonsai trees as dividers
- Moroccan mint tea served here while waiting (complimentary)
- This is the Instagram moment. This is where people post from. Design it that way.

**ZONE 3 — The Greenhouse (Main Dining) — OPEN FLOOR**
- The heart of TREéS. 100+ covers. Open floor plan — no booths blocking sightlines
- Retractable glass roof panels — open sky when weather permits, greenhouse warmth when closed
- Vertical hydroponic gardens on structural columns — herbs, microgreens, edible flowers. Chefs harvest tableside.
- Seating mix: communal long tables (reclaimed wood, 12-tops), intimate 2-tops and 4-tops, elevated banquette seating along the perimeter
- Floor: warm terracotta tile, grounding the space
- Ceiling: Christmas-light constellation pattern (from original concept) — thousands of warm LEDs creating a star field above the plants
- Natural sunlight by day floods in from floor-to-ceiling windows on both sides + glass roof
- By night: warm amber uplighting on plants, constellation ceiling, candlelight on tables
- **Kitchen is VISIBLE** — open pass-through, flames and plating visible from 60% of seats. The kitchen is theater.
- Sound: ambient, warm, conversations blend with gentle music from the stage zone

**ZONE 4 — The Stage & Live Music**
- One stage. Built into the back-center of the main floor — not a separate room
- Raised platform (18in) with retractable acoustic panels for sound shaping
- Visible from every seat in the greenhouse dining area — the music IS the ambiance
- Sound system: intimate acoustic sets through full 5-piece band
- Rotating artist schedule — local Miami musicians + touring acts
- Open mic Wednesdays, live bands Friday-Saturday, jazz brunch Sunday
- Art installations flank both sides of the stage — quarterly rotation, partnership with Wynwood galleries
- Dance floor area (removable chairs) for weekend late-night sets

**ZONE 5 — The Smoke Room (Cannabis Lounge)**
- Tucked behind the stage, naturally separated by the music zone — you walk THROUGH the experience to get here
- Separated HVAC: industrial carbon filtration, negative pressure, isolated ductwork
- The transition: glass-and-plant partition wall, not a solid wall. You see through to the greenhouse but the air doesn't cross.
- Moroccan-inspired: floor cushions, U-shaped low couches, brass tables, ornate textiles
- Hookah + cannabis (joints, vaporizers, edibles — no combustion bongs per FL indoor air regs)
- Digital strain board: interactive screens showing terpene profiles, effects, food pairings (Leafly API)
- Mood lighting: warm amber, dimmable pendant lanterns, no overhead fluorescents
- Capacity: 30-40 seats
- This zone is the reward — the deepest point of the journey. Most intimate. Most private. Most premium.

**ZONE 6 — Private Dining**
- Accessed from the Gallery zone (Zone 2) — before you enter the main floor
- 2 rooms, 8-12 seats each
- Smart glass walls: transparent (part of the energy) or opaque (total privacy) — one toggle
- Pre-fixe menu + dedicated server
- Premium cannabis pairing menu available
- A/V for corporate presentations
- The only enclosed spaces in the entire building

**ZONE 7 — The Outdoor Patio (ONE, massive)**
- Wraps the entire back and right side of the building — 2,000+ sqft
- Accessible from main dining AND the Smoke Room — flow in both directions
- Pergola structure with climbing vines (jasmine, bougainvillea) creating natural shade
- Seating: full dining tables (same menu as inside) + lounge seating (fire pit, daybeds)
- Fire pit lounge at the far end — evening gathering point
- String lights + lanterns throughout
- Designated outdoor smoking area (separate from fire pit seating)
- Water feature: small reflecting pool or fountain — sound of water adds to the nature immersion
- This is where 40% of your Instagram content comes from. Every angle is photogenic.

**STATE-OF-THE-ART KITCHEN**
- Open to the dining floor (pass-through window, 20ft wide)
- Custom hood system with living herb garden integrated above the line
- Dedicated stations: grill/smoke, wok, pastry, garde manger, expediting
- Walk-in connects to greenhouse harvest area — chefs pull ingredients 30 feet from where they're grown
- Designed for theater: flames, plating, movement. Guests watch the art happen.

### Flow Summary
```
STREET → Canopy Entrance
         ├── LEFT: Cafe & Juicery (grab-and-go, morning crowd)
         └── STRAIGHT: Gallery & Waiting Lounge (art wall, tea, buy art)
                       └── FORWARD: Greenhouse Dining (open floor, 100+ seats, visible kitchen)
                                    ├── CENTER: Live Music Stage (visible from all seats)
                                    │           └── BEHIND STAGE: Smoke Room (cannabis lounge)
                                    ├── RIGHT/BACK: Outdoor Patio (dining + lounge + fire pit)
                                    └── SIDE: Private Dining (accessed from Gallery zone)
```

### Sightlines (Every Angle Considered)
- From entrance: you see art wall, greenhouse dining, kitchen flames, stage
- From any dining seat: you see plants, stage, kitchen, other diners, patio through glass
- From stage: musicians face the entire dining floor + patio through glass doors
- From patio: you see the stage through glass, the greenhouse above, the sky
- From Smoke Room: intimate, lower ceiling, warm — deliberate contrast to the open greenhouse

## 4. Cuisine

### Fusion Philosophy
Caribbean-Asian-North African with a seed-oil-free, organic foundation.

**Core pillars:**
- Everything organic where possible
- Zero seed oils — cook with avocado oil, coconut oil, ghee, olive oil, tallow
- Greenhouse-grown herbs and microgreens used in every dish
- Seasonal rotating specials
- Rohan Johnson's creative direction on all menu development

### Menu Architecture

**Starters / Small Plates**
- Jerk chicken spring rolls with scotch bonnet aioli
- Chicken tikka sliders on brioche with mango chutney
- Haitian pikliz deviled eggs
- Plantain chips with three dips (guac, jerk hummus, coconut curry)
- Ackee and saltfish crostini
- House chips — thick-cut, seasoned varieties: jerk spice, curry, BBQ, sea salt & vinegar (served in branded bags, a signature item)

**Mains**
- Oxtail ragu over hand-pulled noodles with bok choy
- Whole roasted jerk chicken with coconut rice and festival bread
- Griot (Haitian fried pork) with pikliz and djon djon rice
- Soy-glazed salmon with wok-fried vegetables and jasmine rice
- Chicken tikka masala bowl with naan and raita
- Crispy duck with tamarind glaze, cassava fries, and pickled slaw
- Seasonal fish — market price, preparation rotates weekly

**Sides**
- Coconut rice & peas
- Mac and cheese (3-cheese, no seed oils)
- Fried plantains (maduros)
- Wok-fried greens with garlic and chili
- Festival bread (Jamaican sweet fried dough)

**Desserts**
- Rum cake with coconut cream
- Mango sticky rice
- Sweet potato pudding
- Matcha panna cotta

**Beverages — Non-Alcoholic**
- Moroccan mint tea service (all day, bottomless during lounge hours)
- Raw pressed juices: green detox, beet ginger, tropical sunrise
- Wellness shots: turmeric, ginger-lemon, activated charcoal
- House-made ginger beer, sorrel, mauby

**Beverages — Alcoholic**
- Craft cocktails with Caribbean spirits (Appleton, Wray & Nephew, Barbancourt)
- Sake and Japanese whisky selection
- Natural wine list
- Local craft beer

**Cannabis Pairings** (Smoke Room exclusive)
- Each main dish has a recommended strain pairing
- Terpene-matched: myrcene-heavy strains with earthy dishes, limonene strains with citrus-forward plates
- Edible dessert options (THC-infused, clearly labeled, dosed)
- Pre-rolls available for purchase

## 5. Technology Integration

### Guest-Facing
- **TREéS App** — order from table, view live menu with greenhouse harvest status, strain info, music schedule, loyalty points
- **QR table ordering** — no paper menus, dynamic pricing for happy hour
- **Strain explorer** — interactive touch screens in Smoke Room showing terpene profiles, effects, food pairings (Leafly API integration)
- **Smart glass** — private dining rooms toggle transparency
- **Dynamic lighting** — circadian-aligned, shifts warm throughout the day automatically

### Operations
- **POS:** Toast or Square for Restaurants
- **Inventory:** real-time greenhouse yield tracking — kitchen knows what's harvestable today
- **HVAC:** separate systems for Smoke Room (negative pressure, carbon filtration) and main dining
- **Reservation:** Resy or OpenTable integration + in-app booking
- **Cannabis compliance:** seed-to-sale tracking integration (Metrc or BioTrackTHC — FL state requirement)
- **Music:** Sonos multi-zone audio, different vibes per zone

### What RAMICHE Already Builds That Plugs In
- **AI agent infrastructure** — could power the recommendation engine (strain + food pairing AI)
- **Parallax design system** — brand identity, visual direction, merch design
- **Galactik Antics aesthetic** — the futuristic-meets-organic visual language is TREéS DNA
- **Content pipeline** — social media engine for daily content (kitchen cam, greenhouse timelapse, live music clips)
- **Stripe integration** — payments, subscriptions for loyalty/membership programs

## 6. Market & Demographics

### Target Location
**Wynwood, Miami** — near art galleries and nightlife, not on the main strip (lower rent, intentional destination feel).

Ideal: side street 1-2 blocks off NW 2nd Avenue. 3,000-5,000 sq ft ground floor with patio space. Adjacent or attached greenhouse structure (or built out from existing warehouse footprint).

### Primary Demographics
| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Creative Professionals | Wynwood's core — designers, artists, tech workers. 25-40. Value aesthetics and experience over price. |
| Health-Conscious Foodies | Drawn by no-seed-oil, organic, farm-to-table. Willing to pay premium. 28-45. |
| Cannabis Consumers | Existing FL medical card holders + recreational market (when legal). 21-50. |
| Corporate Diners | Private dining for client entertainment. Expense account crowd. 30-55. |
| Tourists & Influencers | Wynwood draws 1M+ visitors/year. Instagram-ready spaces drive organic content. |
| Nightlife Crowd | Live music + lounge atmosphere pulls post-dinner/pre-club traffic. 21-35. |

### Competitive Positioning
TREéS occupies a space no one in Miami currently owns: **premium fusion dining + licensed cannabis lounge + live music venue + greenhouse farm-to-table** — all under one roof. Competitors do one or two of these. No one does all four.

| Competitor | What They Do | What TREéS Does Better |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| KYU Wynwood | Asian-fusion, premium | No cannabis, no live music, no greenhouse |
| Kush Wynwood | Cannabis-themed, casual | Theme only — no actual consumption, basic food |
| The Wynwood Yard (closed) | Multi-vendor outdoor space | Single brand, cohesive vision, higher quality |
| Cafe La Trova | Cuban cocktails, live music | Narrow cuisine, no cannabis, no farm concept |

## 7. Cannabis Licensing & Legal

### Florida Status (2026)
- **Medical cannabis:** Legal since 2016 (Amendment 2)
- **Recreational:** Amendment 3 received 57% in Nov 2024 (needed 60%). Likely on 2026 ballot.
- **Cannabis lounges:** Not yet legal in FL. Must monitor legislative session.

### Licensing Strategy
1. **Phase 1 (Pre-legalization):** Open as restaurant + lounge WITHOUT cannabis. Establish the brand, food, music, community.
2. **Phase 2 (Post-legalization):** Apply for cannabis consumption lounge license immediately. Convert Smoke Room to licensed space. Partner with licensed dispensary for product supply.
3. **Dispensary ownership:** Original concept mentioned "Alan Trees (Botanist)" for own dispensary. Evaluate whether vertical integration makes sense vs. partnership. FL dispensary licenses are expensive ($40-60M+ historically for MMTC license). Partnership is more realistic.

### Compliance Requirements
- Separate ventilation (HVAC isolated for Smoke Room)
- Age verification (21+ for Smoke Room)
- Seed-to-sale tracking integration
- No consumption visible from public right-of-way
- Staff training for responsible service
- Insurance: GL + cannabis-specific coverage

### Revenue Streams

|--------|-------------------|

| Bar / Cocktails | 15% |

| Live Music Cover / Tickets | 2% |

### Pricing Architecture
- **Average check (dinner):** $45-65/person
- **Cafe/Juicery:** $8-18/item
- **Smoke Room entry:** free with dining, $20 cover for lounge-only
- **Cannabis products:** market rate + 30% house markup
- **Private dining:** $500-1500 minimum spend
- **Membership program:** $99/month — priority reservations, strain discounts, members-only events

### Unit Economics Target
- **Monthly revenue:** $250K-400K (at capacity)
- **Food cost:** 28-32%
- **Labor:** 30-35%
- **Rent:** 8-10% (Wynwood secondary street)
- **Target margin:** 15-20% EBITDA

## 11. Branding

### Name
**TREéS** — the accent on the second "e" is intentional. Trees = nature, growth, cannabis. The accent = elevated, international, not basic.

### Brand Identity Needed
- Logo (wordmark + icon — a stylized tree or leaf)
- Color palette (earth tones + one accent: forest green, terracotta, sand, warm gold)
- Typography (organic serif + clean sans-serif)
- Packaging (takeout containers, chip bags, merch)
- Merch line: hats, tees, rolling trays, branded lighters, tote bags

### Social Strategy
- Instagram-first — the space IS the content
- Greenhouse timelapse content (daily)
- Chef's table series (short-form video)
