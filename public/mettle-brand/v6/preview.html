Symbolism
The Crown M
The M is the athlete — standing, faceted by discipline. Two legs grounded. Two inner faces: scarlet for the effort, purple for the royalty within.

The three gold crown points rise from the peaks — not placed on top, but emerging from the structure itself. The crown isn't given. It's forged from the M's own geometry.

Three luminous points at each crown tip — the moments of breakthrough. When effort becomes mastery.

Gold edges trace every line — the refining fire that shapes the whole.

Brand Direction
Vee's Notes
Vee — Brand & Positioning
What works: The crown-as-M is strong — it's a dual-read symbol (crown AND letter) which is the hallmark of iconic logos. The biblical colors give it gravitas without being overtly religious. Gold edges = premium positioning. This reads "championship" immediately.

Positioning note: This logo should never feel "cute" or "gamified." The brand's gamification lives in the product, not the identity. The logo should feel like something you'd see on a stadium wall, an Under Armour collab, or a championship ring. Earned, not given.

Usage guidance: Always on dark backgrounds for maximum impact. On light backgrounds, use the deepened variant. Never on busy photos without a solid backing plate. Minimum size: 24px for icon, 100px for full lockup.
